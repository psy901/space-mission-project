October 4, 1957

Soviet Union launches Sputnik 1, the 1st artificial satellite in space (359 miles)

November 3, 1957

Laika the dog becomes the the 1st animal in space in Sputnik 2 (610 miles)

July 29, 1958

USA establishes NASA, starting the space race.

April 12, 1961

Yuri Gagarin becomes the first man in space (327 miles)

September 12, 1962

President John F. Kennedy gives his famous ‘We choose to go to the moon’ speech, spurring USA onwards to the space race.

April 24, 1967

Vladamir Komarov, a Soviet astronaut, becomes the 1st death in space due to a failed parachute with his Soyuz1.

December 21, 1968

Nasa launches Apollo 8, the first manned spacecraft to leave low Earth orbit & into the lunar orbit

The Soviet N1 rocket booster explodes on the launchpad. The rocket hit the pad after an engine shutdown, destroying itself and the launch facility.

USA launches the Apollo 11 mission, with crew members Neil Armstrong, Michael Collins, Edwin E. "Buzz" Aldrin.
